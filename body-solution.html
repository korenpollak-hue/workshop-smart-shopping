Default Mode Network (DMN)

🌀

Mind Wandering

The posterior cingulate (part of the DMN) remains active during tasks in ADHD — causing difficulty focusing and time blindness.

In neurotypical brains, the DMN deactivates during tasks.
In ADHD brains, it stays on — competing with attention networks.

McCormick, E.M., Telzer, E.H. (2018). Contributions of DMN stability to adolescent task engagement. Sci Rep 8.
